Introduction

Electronic music has become a part of our daily lives. It has infested our day the same way technology has. Those 2 terms depend on each other.

The birth of electronic music could not have been possible without the development of technology. You cant make bread without making wheat first.

Electronic music is one of the many children the technology has. It happens to be the creative one, this is what makes it so interesting.

How does it work?

How can a computer create sound? As far as I know, electricity does not have a sound, and even when it does, it is in a form of thunderstruck or electricity cracking (Both not good and certainly not enjoyable).

Electronic music has evolved to such an extent that today, you can't even tell if the sound is “Real” anymore.

Explained

Electronic music is created the same way any other digital form is. In the pure essence of it, it is very simple.

It works on zeros and ones, as most of the technology does. What is interesting about it is the way it works. Imagine electricity running. It forms a wavelength. The main parts of a wave are amplitude and wavelength.

“Amplitude, in physics, is the maximum displacement or distance moved by a point on a vibrating body or wave”.

It can be represented as a 1, or a peak of a wave. Amplitude by itself is not that interesting. When a wave comes to that point, it can be recorded and you can hear a sound from it. A pop sound can be heard. The part that makes it interesting is the wavelength.

These amplitudes are equally distributed in space, so if you manage to control the length of them, you can, in theory, create a repeating sound and create a rhythm.

If you shorten the wavelength to the point that there is no hearable transition between two peaks, you can create a sound. The fast oscillations of electricity is what creates a purely digital sound.

This is just the beginning of the electronic sound. That signal is processed and manipulated many times so that you can achieve the sound you want.
