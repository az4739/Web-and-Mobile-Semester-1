BlueMonday

Blue Monday has become a cultural icon of electronic music. One of the first songs that implement the kind of electronic music that shapes the future. Everyone knows Blue Monday, not by song name but by the recognisable kick it starts with.

Fully electronic and digitally produced, these kicks have a certain repetition cycle that is notable and has been used many times after the initial release of Blue Monday.

The new order is a band famous for the implementation of different genres, music instruments and different ideas in their music. One of the most influential sounds from that period of music can be heard from blue Monday.

As the music scene was changing, New order was changing with it. Clubs became bigger, sound systems developed and the demand for bigger and stronger music was up. The music of that time could not keep up with the quantity of decibels speakers could produce at the time. The audience was a story to itself.

Dancers on the dancefloor could not keep to themselves and were never satisfied. In times like these, the New order decided to create an “old-new” song. The song was almost completely created from electronic sounds yet played the old fashion way. The bass flows naturally as if it was played with a real instrument. Still, the sound of it is purely electronic, distorted and most importantly, new. The impact of this was huge.

It appealed to both audiences at the time, the young hyper generations that were coming and demanded new sound, and the older generations who can still hear the familiar basslines under all that electronic noise. This was satisfying because we as humans look for repetition, and as species like the familiar things.

is important to notice that the song itself was groovy heaven. People from all over the world could dance to its beats without knowing the lyrics. This was achieved by, again the electronic rhythm machine that was responsible for replacing the drums. All in all, the song was and still is a mega-hit that connected the world. Its magical sounds can be heard and recognised all over the globe and it led many new artists to develop their sound inspired by Blue Monday.

Freed From Desire

A hit song by Gala, Freed from desire had a huge impact on modern electronic music for a different reason from New orders Blue Monday. The arrangement. Freed from desire was a hit song because of its famous vocals and the interesting humming that was so universal everyone could enjoy it.

The importance of this song in history can be found in its arrangement of the parts of the song. The most famous part of the song, the vocals are not as important as the impact it had on the people. Without knowing about it, the listeners were presented with a new, not so standard, arrangement of modern electronic music. This arrangement was built with one thing in mind.

Easier mixing. Again, the new ages were coming and there was a demand for change in standard music. The most famous way of performing in a club was a Djing. Still, DJs had a problem, the songs they had at that time were short, snappy and fast. (Learn more about this on the Mixing for dummies page of this website)

There was no time for a Dj to perform because the songs were going fast and the Dj could not keep up. Freed from desire changed this. Gala saw the struggles Djs have with short songs so they decides to change this. A long intro with only the beat can be heard as the beginning of the song, this is not usual because the intro of the song is the one that will buy the audience attention.

This decision was made to help Djs perform their song in the most important places for the music scene at the time, the clubs. To get back the attention of the audience from that long and boring intro, Freed from desire has recognisable, relatable and easy to learn lyrics.

All combined, Gala and Freed from desire managed to control the audience and learn them to love things that the industry needed at that time. Nowadays, Freed from desire is considered to be one of the most influential songs that at the same time capture the feel of transition from analog to digital and yet force the change to its listeners.
